How Audio Influences the Simulation

Bass Frequencies (Low)

Control cell birth rates and create random births. Strong bass causes more cells to spontaneously appear, creating explosive growth patterns that mirror the rhythmic foundation of your music.

Mid Frequencies (Medium)

Modify survival rules by adjusting the minimum and maximum neighbor requirements. Mid-range frequencies create flowing, organic patterns as they alter the classic Conway's Game of Life rules.

High Frequencies (Treble)

Generate mutations and random state flips. High frequencies add sparkle and chaos to the simulation, creating unexpected bursts of activity that reflect the brightness in your audio.

Genre Presets

Each genre preset adjusts how the frequencies influence the simulation:

  • Electronic: Enhanced bass and treble response for sharp, digital patterns
  • Classical: Mid-frequency emphasis with gentle mutations for flowing, organic growth
  • Rock: Balanced response with increased mutation rates for dynamic patterns
  • Jazz: Mid-heavy with moderate complexity for intricate, evolving structures
  • Ambient: Treble-focused with slow mutations for ethereal, drifting patterns
